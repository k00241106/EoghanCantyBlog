Entry 1 for October

Eoghan Canty Fri 15/10, 2021

Welcome to my first entry of October. Every month from now I will continue to add a total of three blog entries per month which will document my progress on the final year project. After September I clearly understood what was expected of us to develop a 4th year standard based project. As I would like to achieve a first class honours degree I would have to incorporate a lot of features and technologies within my project. Because of the complexity of this project it would be vital but also a necessity to develop a gantt chart. According to ProjectManager.com, a gantt chart is a bar chart that provides a visual view of project tasks scheduled over time. It essentially used for project planning. Gantt charts are developed using either Microsoft Excel, Google Sheets or any project management software. In my case I will be developing it on Microsoft Excel. This is what I am most comfortable using. When developing the gantt chart I broke down the project into the main stages of the entire project. These stages are the research report, design presentation, the Christmas period, presentation of first prototype, technical work, posters, videos, articles, final report and the final demonstration. I then broke down the main stages into sub-stages to give myself a more accurate representation of what had to be done during each stage of the project. For the research report which is coming up next I broke this section down into market research, technologies research, design research, the writing of the report and the delivery of the report. For the design presentation, I broke this part down into logo design, design system, the UI design, making the presentation, and delivering the presentation. For the Christmas run in I hope to get done the HTML pages, a skeleton scene for the AR app and a few models for the AR app. After the Christmas break I want to: build the ecommerce part of the site, to allow the user to display one car and informational text box in the AR app and finally publishing 1 article to the website. After the prototype I plan to fully build out the back end of the website, finish the AR app and finally deploy the website to a live server. Once all of the technical work has finished I plan to make posters and deliver them. After this I need to publish four fully written articles and publish 4 fully edited videos. For the penultimate stage of the project I need to write and upload the final FYP report. To conclude the FYP project I will need to prepare a presentation and also demonstrate it in front of supervisors and external examiners.

Overall, creating the gantt chart that is seen above was a great experience. Even though it seems quite boring it gave me a structure to my project that I did not have. The project schedule has clearly defined tasks which are appropriately spaced out between each other without piling up on me either.
